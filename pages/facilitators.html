What Facilitators Need To Do

Use this quick guide to support teams, rotate fairly and alert the secretariat when a group needs help.

Rotate · Track · Alert

Before Rotation

  • Check your assigned programme block
  • Confirm your team numbers
  • Check whether you have roving duty
  • Be in position before the session starts

During Team Support

  • Ask guiding questions
  • Check visible outputs and progress
  • Keep teams focused on the current phase
  • Avoid giving teams direct solutions

Before Phase Ends

  • Check the required templates
  • Confirm mahjong paper outcomes
  • Verify Smart DT uploads
  • Flag incomplete work before rotation

Alert Secretariat

  • Report delayed or stuck teams
  • Raise welfare or group-conflict concerns
  • Report missing materials
  • Escalate technical or venue issues

Programme Flow & Facilitator Rotation

Facilitators work in pairs, follow the programme outputs and rotate across teams alternately. One pair acts as roving support in each block.

Rotation principle: Each facilitator pair supports different teams across the programme blocks. Roving support should help delayed teams, movement, logistics, welfare checks and urgent team needs.

Your Role at CAMP21

Three core duties across all four days.

Monitor Team Progress

  • Walk between team clusters during active work
  • Note the phase each group is executing
  • Check progress against the current activity
  • Flag teams that are stuck or off-track

Guide Checkpoint Sign-offs

  • Check outputs at every phase transition
  • Follow Empathy → Define → Ideate → Prototype → Test
  • Confirm minimum template requirements are met
  • Allow teams to advance only after the checkpoint

Support Logistics

  • Assist with material distribution
  • Support room transitions and printing requests
  • Respond to venue-related needs
  • Coordinate urgent matters with the secretariat table
