Mahjong Paper Outcome Guide

Trainer reference for the exact mahjong paper / visual output expected from participants in each phase.

⌄
Trainer Reminder: Please guide students based on the exact activity outcomes below. Do not add extra mahjong paper requirements unless instructed by the secretariat.

Empathy Phase

T04

Activity: Mahjong Paper — T04 Empathy Map

  • Students produce the Empathy Map outcome on mahjong paper.
  • The output should support the Empathy phase evidence and discussion.
  • Trainers check that the outcome is clear, readable and aligned with T04.

Define Phase

T06

Activity: Mahjong Paper — T05 User Persona + T06 User Insight & Needs Statement / Design Statement

  • Students produce the Persona, Need and Design Statement outcome.
  • The output should show the team’s defined user and problem direction.
  • Trainers check that the outcome is aligned with T06.

Ideate Phase

T08–T09

Activity: Mahjong Paper — T08 & T09

  • T08: SCAMPER
  • T09: Idea Prioritisation Matrix
  • Trainers check that both ideation outcomes are visible and understandable.

Prototype Phase

T11

Activity: Mahjong Paper / Canva / PPT / Storyboard — Full Prototype Outcome for T11 (Optional)

  • Students may use mahjong paper, Canva, PPT or storyboard format.
  • The chosen format should present the full prototype outcome for T11.
  • Trainers guide students to choose the format that best represents their prototype.

Test & Reflect Phase

T12

Activity: Mahjong Paper — User Feedback Form T12

  • Students produce the User Feedback Form outcome on mahjong paper.
  • The output should record feedback clearly for the Test & Reflect phase.
  • Trainers check that the outcome is aligned with T12 User Feedback Form.
Important: These are the official mahjong paper / visual output expectations. Prototype outcome for T11 is optional in mahjong paper format because students may use Canva, PPT or storyboard instead.

Trainer Assistant Role & Escalation Protocol

Clarifies what trainer assistants should track, support and report during the programme.

⌄

Trainer Assistant Role

Support the lead trainer and keep group movement organised.

1
Support group movement and time reminders.
2
Help check whether templates and mahjong paper outcomes are completed.
3
Remind groups to upload evidence to Smart DT Guide & Template Hub.
4
Report stuck or delayed groups to the lead trainer.
5
Do not approve phase completion without lead trainer confirmation.

When To Alert Secretariat

Escalate early so issues can be solved before the phase ends.

!
Group is more than 30 minutes behind schedule.
!
Group has no clear problem statement after Define.
!
Group starts prototype without T10 sign-off.
!
Smart DT Guide & Template Hub or Google Drive access issue occurs.
!
Group conflict, inactive members or participant welfare issue occurs.
Important: Trainer assistants help monitor and support, but final phase approval should come from the assigned lead trainer or secretariat instruction.

Day 1 + Day 2 + Day 3 reflection evidence.

Important Separate Marks

Not part of Final Pitch 100% evaluation.

Trainer Reminder: Evaluate the reflection quality, learning evidence and communication clarity. This challenge is not about producing the most perfect video; it is about showing genuine daily learning and Design Thinking progress.
🎥 TikTok Challenge Guide

Format & Submission Instructions

3 Daily Videos
Duration1–3 minutes

One short reflection video per team for each programme day.

Video SizeVertical · 9:16

Record in portrait TikTok format with clear visuals and readable captions.

LanguageEnglish / Bilingual

Use English or bilingual English–Malay communication.

SubmissionDay 1, Day 2 & Day 3

Submit each daily reflection through the official channel before the stated deadline.

1

Show the team’s Design Thinking progress and completed daily outputs.

2

Explain key learning, user insights, challenges and improvements.

3

Include visible evidence from Smart DT templates or activities.

4

Protect privacy, obtain consent and use appropriate music and captions.

Day 1

Empathy + Define Reflection

Focus on observation framework, interview insights, empathy map, persona, user needs and problem direction.

Day 2

Ideate + Prototype Plan Reflection

Focus on 3D Circle Challenge, SCAMPER, Idea Prioritisation Matrix, selected idea and prototype direction.

Day 3

Prototype + Test & Reflect Reflection

Focus on prototype outcome, feedback capture, improvement plan and pitch preparation evidence.

15/50

Daily Design Thinking Reflection 15/50 marks

Shows clear reflection from Day 1, Day 2 and Day 3 activities, linked to the correct DT phase and team progress.

15/50

Learning, Insight & Evidence 15/50 marks

Explains meaningful learning, user insight, challenges, improvement and completed evidence from the Smart DT templates.

10/50

Teamwork, Communication & Clarity 10/50 marks

The team communicates clearly, shares roles fairly and presents the reflection in a way that is easy to understand.

10/50

Creativity, Ethics & TikTok Presentation 10/50 marks

Video is creative, respectful, suitable for TikTok format, uses appropriate captions/music and follows ethical recording practice.

Total TikTok Reflection Challenge Evaluation: 50 raw marks × 2 = 100%
